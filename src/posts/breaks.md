## An unexpected read

Along my reading journey, I have read very few sci-fi books, being the ones I loved the most the *Metro* saga from Dmitry Glukhovsky and *Roadside Picnic* from the Strugatski Brothers. While I started reading the first ones after discovering the games(which I haven't played yet), I knew about *Roadside Picnic* because it was one of the reads that my Philosophy professor from high school recommended. Anyway, both present a desolated world where humanity has to fight to survive, with very few resources and constant hazards, which leads to a fragmented and hostile society that usually leaves you abandoned against any threat.

On the other hand, I discovered the Three Body Problem universe thanks to a recommendation from a friend from China. I saw that it was on sale on Apple Books, and at that moment I didn´t have any other physical books to be read, so I decided to give it a go. 

## Philosophical Thoughts and Dilemmas

At the beginning, I couldn't imagine how a mistery of missing scientists could lead to what was going to happen in the following chapters. The concept from which the Trisolaran World comes is the mathematical problem of the Three Bodies, a chaotic system where it is not possible to predict the position of the bodies at any time. If this happened in a Solar System, instead of regular seasons, the planet suffers constant and unpredictable changes in the weather, the barrier between an ice-cold day and a scorching one were mere seconds. Under this conditions, a civilization that managed to thrive could be considered one that managed to deal with this system, one that curiously was incredibly advanced.

From that on, the plot changes entirely, instead of discovering a whole new world, our world, the Earth, is under possibly one of the biggest threats in it's entire history, an extraterrestrial civilization is travelling to this planet with the purpose of invading it and settling down in a more stable world.

In this moment of chaos, the world decides to sort of unite, and they desperately create the Wallfacer program, where four individuals were given unlimited and unrestricted power to do whatever they want, these four people have to design a strategy that cannot be revealed in any way, since these Trisolarans are actually listening to everything that is happening in the Earth via the Sophons, which are supercomputers with the size of a proton. From that moment, they try to design different strategies and plans. Most of them will fail to do so, incapable of dealing with such reality, until one of the protagonists develops a theory that describes a dark and raw reality of the universe.

After this, the human civilization goes through a series of events in a lapse of several centuries where everything was possible, from the total extermination of the civilization, to the idea of a human exploration of the universe beyond the Solar System.

What impacted me from this book where two characters that went through a vast amount of dilemmas due to the amount of responsibilities they carried on their shoulders: Luo Ji and Cheng Xin.

The first one had all the powers on the world, yet, the first thing he did was to spend several years living in peace in an isolated house in the middle of nowhere, as if he didn't care at all about the human extintion. And this raises the question: What would you do in this situation, would you have tried to focus on developing the technology, or creating a plan that could fool the Trisolarans, or, given the fact that the chances of survival were at the lowest, would you have gone wild and spend all the money to live as you wished?

The second one, after being given the role of Swordholder, faced the situation in which she could either reveal the position of the enemies even if it involved revealing the location of the Earth too to provoke the extintion of both worlds, or refuse to do so and risk it all, allowing the Trisolarans to subdue human civilization, with the hope that in a future they could manage to survive all these events. Thus, the question is: Given the case that you could die under the attack of someone(or something), would you prefer that you both were exterminated, or would you accept your fate and simply die?

## An image of the society

Throughout the Trilogy, human civilization faces events that lead to the best and the worst of it. From a civilized world where peace was onmipresent and everyone enjoyed the best of conditions, to a civilization that was forced to relocate to a single continent and fight against each other for their own survival, the humans went through many events where they couldn't see a bright future, but still, they decided to be comfident about it, and never lost the hope, even in the last of their days.
